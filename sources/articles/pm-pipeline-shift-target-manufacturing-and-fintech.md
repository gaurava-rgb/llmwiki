The PM pipeline isn't dead... 𝘪𝘵 𝘫𝘶𝘴𝘵 𝘮𝘰𝘷𝘦𝘥.

MBAs are competing for fewer spots in Big Tech, while entire industries are being overlooked.

[Maureen May](<https://www.linkedin.com/in/maureenmay?trk=public_post-text>), Head of Employer Relations & Recruiting at [Carnegie Mellon University - Tepper School of Business](<https://www.linkedin.com/school/carnegie-mellon-tepper-school-of-business/?trk=public_post-text>), put it bluntly: "The PM function, unfortunately, has been flooded in the job market with an overabundance of talent."

If you're an MBA targeting PM roles at FAANG companies, you're competing against thousands of experienced PMs who were laid off from those exact companies. The odds aren't in your favor.

However, there's opportunity elsewhere.

𝐓𝐡𝐞 𝐛𝐞𝐬𝐭 𝐏𝐌 𝐨𝐩𝐩𝐨𝐫𝐭𝐮𝐧𝐢𝐭𝐢𝐞𝐬 𝐫𝐢𝐠𝐡𝐭 𝐧𝐨𝐰 𝐦𝐢𝐠𝐡𝐭 𝐧𝐨𝐭 𝐛𝐞 𝐢𝐧 𝐭𝐞𝐜𝐡.

Think manufacturing, robotics, and automation. These industries need people who can bridge business strategy and tech implementation... and they're not always flooded with applicants.

"An area that MBAs should probably revisit is the manufacturing space. That is where a ton of innovation is and will be happening via robotics," May explains.

Financial services is another pocket. Banks and fintech companies are hiring technical talent, and the competition looks different than consumer tech.

𝐈𝐟 𝐲𝐨𝐮'𝐫𝐞 𝐬𝐞𝐞𝐤𝐢𝐧𝐠 𝐏𝐌 𝐫𝐨𝐥𝐞𝐬 𝐩𝐨𝐬𝐭-𝐌𝐁𝐀:

➕ Target industries where your tech background helps you stand out
➕ Look at manufacturing, robotics, and fintech companies
➕ Consider more senior PM roles in emerging industries vs. APM roles at saturated Big Tech companies

Your technical credentials give you MORE of an advantage in non-tech industries. For example, in manufacturing or financial services, you're not always competing with hundreds of candidates with the same background.
