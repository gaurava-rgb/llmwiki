[Meta Platforms](<https://www.theinformation.com/org-charts/meta?rc=c48ukx>) is hiring four more OpenAI artificial intelligence researchers as Meta CEO Mark Zuckerberg continues an unprecedented recruiting effort to catch up to his AI rivals.

The researchers, Shengjia Zhao, Jiahui Yu, Shuchao Bi and Hongyu Ren, have each agreed to join, according to a person familiar with their hiring. They follow four other OpenAI researchers whose hirings were reported a few days ago. Their hirings were made after Meta’s agreement earlier this month to pay $14.3 billion to take a 49% stake in Scale AI and hire its CEO Alexandr Wang to lead its “superintelligence” efforts.

Zuckerberg has aggressively overhauled his AI teams following the lackluster launch in April of Llama 4, the latest version of the company’s flagship large language model, which powers the company’s AI chatbots on Facebook, Instagram and WhatsApp.

Zuckerberg had anticipated the model would perform well, but it didn’t. And his team quickly came under fire after uploading an experimental version of the model to a popular leaderboard, which performed better than the version of the model it released to the public.

[OpenAI](<https://www.theinformation.com/org-charts/openai?rc=c48ukx>) has several hundred or so core researchers who develop AI models, and it’s survived such departures before. Last fall, for instance, Chief Technology Officer Mira Murati and other senior research leaders left suddenly, but the company’s [subscription business has boomed this year](<https://www.theinformation.com/articles/ai-native-startups-pass-15-billion-annualized-revenue?rc=c48ukx>) and could blow past its aggressive 2025 revenue target.

The growth of OpenAI’s ChatGPT and other AI services has [ratcheted up the industry’s talent war to new heights lately](<https://www.theinformation.com/articles/inside-great-ai-talent-auction-deals-free-agents-egos?rc=c48ukx>), capped by Meta’s hiring of Wang. Meta is also in advanced talks to hire former GitHub CEO Nat Friedman and Safe Superintelligence CEO Daniel Gross, as well as to partially buy out their venture fund for at least $1 billion.

OpenAI CEO Sam Altman has gone on the offensive, claiming last week in a podcast that Meta was offering $100 million sign-on bonuses to recruits from OpenAI. Meta has told its employees that wasn’t true.

Zhao, one of the new Meta hires from OpenAI, was named in [The Information’s AI Talent Tracker](<https://www.theinformation.com/projects/ai-research-scientists?rc=c48ukx>) earlier this year. He has been a key contributor to OpenAI’s models, including OpenAI’s o1-mini and o3-mini models. The latter are the company’s small, fast reasoning models, which have impressed developers with their performance. Last fall, o1-mini outperformed OpenAI’s larger o1-preview model on math.

Before joining OpenAI in June 2022, Zhao was a doctoral candidate in computer science at Stanford University, according to his LinkedIn profile.

Yu has led an OpenAI team working on perception, or how AI gathers and understands information about its surroundings, according to his LinkedIn profile. Bi is OpenAI’s head of multimodal post-training, according to his LinkedIn profile. Ren, too, was a key contributor to o1-mini and o3-mini. He published a paper with Zhao in 2018 about bias in generative AI models, according to Zhao’s public research profile.

The four researchers went to college in China, according to their LinkedIn profiles.

Over the past week, Meta hired three OpenAI researchers from the company’s Zurich office. The trio work on multimodal AI, or models that can recognize and produce not just text but also video or audio. They joined OpenAI from Google DeepMind late last year. Trapit Bansal, who works on reasoning models that take more time to “think” before responding to a query, is also joining Meta.

OpenAI has let its employees cash out $3 billion worth of shares in the last couple of years by selling them to financial investors who have clamored for a stake in the ChatGPT maker.
