I just open sourced Siftly, a self-hosted AI knowledge base for your X bookmarks.

Most people save thousands of tweets and never use or find them again.

Siftly fixes that with a full AI pipeline that runs locally on your machine.

Here's what it does:

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HCw1sXBaUAAIpSc.jpg?name=orig)

* * *

→ AI vision analysis

reads text, objects, and context inside your bookmarked posts including images and thumbnails

→ Natural language search

finds posts by meaning, not just keywords

→ Smart collections

auto-sorts into pre-built categories plus any custom categories you

* * *

The AI pipeline runs 4 stages automatically with 20 parallel workers:

→ Entity extraction

mines hashtags, URLs, mentions, and 100+ known

tool/product names from raw tweet data

→ Vision analysis

reads every image, GIF, and video thumbnail. generates 30–40 visual tags per image

* * *

Free. Open source. Locally hosted.

If you have Claude Code CLI installed and signed in, the AI features use your existing subscription automatically with no API key setup needed.
