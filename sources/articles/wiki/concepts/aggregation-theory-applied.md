# Aggregation Theory Applied

## Pattern
Distribution owners keep reappearing as the parties with the cleanest leverage over the rest of the stack. Whether the surface is devices, streaming, creator platforms, enterprise software, or ads, the winner is often the one that owns demand and can decide how upstream capability reaches users.

## Instances
- **2026-04** in [[../topics/apple]]: Apple's advantage is framed as control of the device and interface layer.
- **2026-04** in [[../topics/openai-strategy]]: Frontier and ChatGPT expansion are attempts to own more of the user workflow.
- **2026-04** in [[../topics/media-publishing]]: Netflix, YouTube, and the NYT all show different forms of audience-side leverage.
- **2026-04** in [[../topics/ai-industry-economics]]: Ads, aggregation, and monetization debates keep returning to who owns attention.
- **2026-04** in [[../topics/startup-growth]]: Founders in the corpus repeatedly reach for distribution loops before proprietary tech.

## What This Means
Model capability matters, but the corpus keeps showing that distribution is where margin and durability concentrate. Aggregation remains one of the most reusable lenses in the wiki.
