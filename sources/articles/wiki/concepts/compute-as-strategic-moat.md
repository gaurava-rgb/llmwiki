# Compute as Strategic Moat

## Pattern
Across the rebuilt corpus, compute is not just a hardware line item; it is the throttle on product quality, enterprise reliability, pricing freedom, and even narrative confidence. The shift is from talking about model intelligence in the abstract to asking who can actually procure, allocate, and productize enough capacity to serve real workloads.

## Instances
- **2026-04** in [[../topics/anthropic-strategy]]: TPU deals and Claude throttling make supply constraints visible to end users.
- **2026-04** in [[../topics/openai-strategy]]: Frontier and productivity ambitions only work if OpenAI can keep capacity available across more surfaces.
- **2026-04** in [[../topics/ai-lab-competition]]: Chips, TPUs, foundries, and cloud capex dominate the competitive discussion.
- **2026-04** in [[../topics/ai-industry-economics]]: Run-rate and pricing stories are inseparable from the cost of serving demand.
- **2026-04** in [[../topics/apple]]: Apple coverage shows the mirror image: owning distribution can matter even when model training is outsourced.

## What This Means
Compute remains a moat, but not a standalone one. It matters most when paired with distribution and workflow control. Labs that can neither secure capacity nor redirect demand will struggle to convert attention into durable advantage.
