# AI Agent Workflows

## Summary [coverage: high -- 40 sources]
Agent workflows are one of the clearest growth areas in the rebuilt wiki. The sources cover orchestration, memory, note-taking agents, automations, autoresearch patterns, and the commercial demand around agents that can execute multi-step tasks instead of merely answering questions. ## Key Thesis [coverage: high -- 40 sources]
Agent value comes from workflow design and state management, not just model strength; the harness determines whether capability becomes useful work.

## Key Insights [coverage: high -- 40 sources]
- Memory, orchestration, and task decomposition are recurring bottlenecks in nearly every agent discussion.
- Services and products are forming around specific agent loops, which suggests the market is moving from demos to operational niches.
- The best workflows in this corpus are explicitly structured: tools are scoped, outputs are checked, and state is preserved.

## Industry Context [coverage: high -- 40 sources]
This topic sits between model strategy and practical tooling. It explains why the corpus now treats agents as operational systems with budgets, error modes, and commercial packaging rather than science-fair novelties.

## Implications [coverage: high -- 40 sources]
- Design the workflow before chasing the fanciest model.
- Invest in repeatable tool interfaces and memory structures.
- Agent products that remove obvious orchestration pain are likely to keep compounding.
