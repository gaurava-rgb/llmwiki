# AI Craft and Quality

## Summary [coverage: high -- 16 sources]
The rebuilt corpus reinforces a strong anti-slop current: AI makes it easier to ship, but it also makes mediocre output instantly abundant. That raises the premium on taste, interface polish, design systems, and the willingness to finish details instead of stopping at the first acceptable result. This rebuild groups 16 sources into this topic. Representative sources include "Vibe Coding, Windsurf and Anthropic, ChatGPT Connectors", "HOW TO VIBE CODE BEAUTIFUL UI", and "The quality of your vibecoded slop is horrible".

## Key Thesis [coverage: high -- 16 sources]
As AI lowers the cost of making things, quality becomes more visible and more valuable; speed is no longer enough to signal competence on its own.

## Key Insights [coverage: high -- 16 sources]
- Vibe-coding critiques are less anti-AI than anti-carelessness: the complaint is not tool use but low standards.
- Design systems keep appearing as leverage because they let teams move fast without producing obvious inconsistency.
- A recurring pattern is that builders who treat output as draft material, not final work, get much better results.

## Industry Context [coverage: high -- 16 sources]
This topic complements the builder-stack and PM topics. The shared lesson is that AI raises the floor, but the ceiling still depends on judgment, review, and craft discipline.

## Implications [coverage: high -- 16 sources]
- Use AI to accelerate iteration, not to suspend standards.
- Codify taste where possible through systems, examples, and review rituals.
- Quality is re-emerging as a strategic differentiator precisely because cheap output is everywhere.
