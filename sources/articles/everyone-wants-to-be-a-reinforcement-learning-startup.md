These days, artificial intelligence developers, investors and founders are all obsessed with **“reinforcement learning,”** a method for improving AI by rewarding it for accomplishing certain goals and penalizing it for other behaviors.

Investors tell me that they’re getting pitched on dozens of RL-related startups per week, ranging from those [building simulations of real apps for models to learn how to use](<https://www.theinformation.com/articles/ai-agenda-startups-hoping-unseat-scale-ai>) to those [aiming to help businesses specialize models on their performance metrics](<https://www.theinformation.com/articles/ex-openai-cto-muratis-startup-plans-compete-openai-others>).

Existing startups are pivoting into this area as well. One example is **LMArena** , a site that pits AI models against each other and lets users vote on which response they like the best. Originally funded through a combination of grants and donations, LMArena raised a $100 million “seed” round in May from **Andreessen Horowitz** and the investment arm of the **University of California**.

By pitting models against each other, LMArena generates data on what users like and dislike about model responses—a crucial part of the reinforcement learning process. For instance, LMArena also offers **“WebDev Arena,”** a benchmark that allows users to provide an idea for a website or app like a “calorie tracker app” and then uses different AI coding assistants to create mock-ups of that app. Then, the user can vote on which mock-up they like best.

That sort of data is extremely valuable to companies making those models, which explains how LMArena has reached several million dollars in annualized revenue in the five months since its launch as a for-profit company, said a person with knowledge of the numbers. It sells that data to model developers, they told me.

Other AI startups are also emphasizing their access to valuable data. Last Thursday, **Anysphere** , the maker of popular coding assistant **Cursor** , [said](<https://x.com/cursor_ai/status/1966264816529723462>) on X it had trained a new version of its code autocomplete model, **Tab** , which makes fewer but better suggestions than the previous model. The company said the model was trained in such a way that it was discouraged from making code suggestions that the user wasn’t likely to accept—i.e., reinforcement learning.

The idea of app builders using customer feedback to improve their products isn’t new. But the marketing push around such strategies from AI app startups is notable, especially given that more companies are both developing AI models and apps powered by those models.

Essentially, Anysphere and other startups are highlighting the fact that they have hard-to-get user data that model makers like **OpenAI** or **Anthropic** —who are both suppliers and competitors of Anysphere—might not have. (That might explain why model makers like OpenAI and **xAI** have shown interest in licensing that user data, as we’ve previously [reported](<https://www.theinformation.com/articles/openai-xai-show-interest-cursors-coding-data>).)

### Deals and Debuts

 _See The Information’s[Generative AI Database](<https://www.theinformation.com/projects/generative-ai>) for an exclusive list of private companies and their investors._

**Lila Sciences** , which is building AI software to advance biology, chemistry and materials research, raised a $235 million Series A funding round co-led by **Braidwell** and **Collective Global**.

**Scintil Photonics** , a French startup aimed at scaling photonics for AI factories, raised €50 million in Series B funding led by **Yotta Capital Partners** and **NGP Capital**.

**MarqVision** , which uses computer vision to fight counterfeiting and trademark infringement, raised $48 million in Series B funding led by **Peak XV Partners**.

**Micro1** , a data labeling startup, raised a $35 million Series A funding round at a $500 million valuation led by **01 Advisors**.

**Conceivable Life Sciences** , which makes a robotic lab to create human embryos, raised $50 million in funding led by **Advance Venture Partners**.

**Brain Co.** , which develops AI-powered enterprise applications, raised $30 million in Series A funding from **Elad Gil’s Gil Capital** and **Jared Kushner's Affinity Partners**.

**Terra Security** , which uses AI to do continuous web app penetration testing, raised $30 million in Series A funding led by **Felicis**.

**Penguin Ai** , which develops AI software for healthcare administration, raised $29.7 million in Series A funding led by **Greycroft**.

**Meela** , a New York City-based developer of an AI voice companion for seniors, raised $3.5 million in seed funding led by **Bain Capital Ventures**.

**Pascal AI** , a Bangalore, India-based AI startup that tracks people’s investments, raised $3.1 million in seed funding led by **Kalaari Capital**.

**OpenAI** said on Thursday that it had reached a nonbinding agreement with **Microsoft** on a revised partnership that would allow the startup to convert its for-profit arm into a public benefit corporation.

**XAI** laid off 500 workers from its data annotation team on Friday night, according to a report from Business Insider.

**Penske Media Corporation** , the parent company of publications including **Rolling Stone** , **Billboard** , **Variety** and **Hollywood Reporter** , sued **Google** , accusing it of illegally using news publishers’ content to create AI summaries that damage their business.

California's state senate approved **SB 53** , a new AI safety bill that would set new transparency requirements on large companies. The bill is now going to **Governor Gavin Newsom** to sign or veto.
