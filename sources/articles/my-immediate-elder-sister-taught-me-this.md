My immediate elder sister taught me this

No matter the Job she gets and no matter the country or high salary

She types her resignation letter down the third month after assumption and she begins to look for a higher Job 6 months after settling in

It's dog eat dog.

And it paid her. her peers are 8 years behind her in terms of career progression. In fact she surpassed many older people in her industry.

My dad would also tell us, if the company is not yours or your investment is not in it, capitalism will kick you out without a tear when it needs to. So place your interests above the firm's interest

If they don't give you a comfortable schedule to upskill, resign. That's how my sister resigned from a bigger pay firm to another firm because of her PhD.

After the PhD she was poached by the firm she resigned from again, and other international organisations poached her in the same period.

It's Capitalism baby,

Red your eyes and show no mercy.

There is a term called career insurance.

The mistakes too many career people make is to wait until they're actively looking before they start positioning themselves.

You only update your LinkedIn profile when you need a job. You optimize your CV only when you see a posting you want to apply for.

But the best opportunities don't work that way anymore.

The strongest opportunities find people who are already visible, current, memorable, well-known, and easy to place. Not the ones scrambling after a posting appears.

Their LinkedIn profile reflects what they do now, not what they did 10 years ago.

And when someone thinks, “Who do we know who could help with this?” their name comes up.

If you’re in a stable role today, this is actually the easiest time to do this work since it's low pressure, high reward.
