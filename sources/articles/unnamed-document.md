Please don’t sit in a PM-level interview at Amazon, Apple, Microsoft, or Meta without preparing for these 7 questions. (They get asked in 90% of the interviews.) 1\. Tell me about a time you launched a product that didn’t go as planned. \- Pick a clear example with measurable impact. \- Show how you adapted and solved the problem. \- End with what you learned and applied later. 2\. How do you decide what features to prioritize? \- Mention your decision-making framework (RICE, MoSCoW, etc.). \- Include both data-driven and customer insights. \- Show how you align with business goals. 3\. Tell me about a time you disagreed with a stakeholder. \- Describe the situation objectively, without blame. \- Explain how you listened, aligned on goals, and found common ground. \- End with a positive resolution and outcome. 4\. How do you measure the success of a product after launch? \- Mention specific metrics (adoption, retention, NPS, revenue). \- Explain how you track them and gather qualitative feedback. \- Show how you use this data for continuous improvement. 5\. Describe a time when you managed conflicting priorities. \- Show your process for assessing urgency and impact. \- Explain how you communicated trade-offs to stakeholders. \- Share the outcome and any long-term improvements. 6\. How do you gather customer feedback? \- Mention multiple channels (user interviews, surveys, analytics). \- Explain how you turn feedback into actionable insights. \- Show an example of how feedback led to a product improvement. 7\. Tell me about a product decision you made with incomplete data. \- Show how you assessed the risk and possible scenarios. \- Explain your reasoning and how you validated the decision later. \- Highlight the result and what you learned about decision-making under uncertainty. These aren’t just “PM” questions; they’re leadership questions. Because at the senior level, companies are testing how you think, not just what you know.
