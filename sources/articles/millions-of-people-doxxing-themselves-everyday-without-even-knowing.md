* * *

A few people asking why Waze has usernames in the first place.

Short answer: Waze was never just a nav app - it was built as a social network for drivers.

The username system is baked into everything.

* * *

The biggest reason was Waze Carpool. From 2016-2022 they ran a full carpooling service where drivers could offer rides to commuters heading the same direction.

You needed usernames so riders and drivers could identify and trust each other.

COVID killed predictable commuting so they shut it down - but the username infrastructure stayed.

* * *

Then there’s the social features most people forget exist.

You can add friends, see them on the map in real-time, send “beep beeps” (basically pokes), and chat with other drivers.

All of this needs persistent identity to work

* * *

There’s also full gamification.

Points for every report, leaderboards showing top contributors in your area, and ranks you level up through - literally called Baby → Grown-up → Warrior → Knight → Royalty.

Your avatar gets visual upgrades (pacifier, shield, sword, crown).

None of this works without usernames tied to your activity.

* * *

And 500,000 volunteers edit Waze maps.

Every road fix, every new business, every corrected speed limit - tied to a username for attribution and accountability.

It’s how they built accurate maps without paying for it.

* * *

The problem is all these features assume you want to be identified.

There’s no middle ground - no way to report traffic anonymously while still participating in the community.

You’re either fully visible or you don’t contribute. And most users have no idea their reports are being broadcast publicly with their username attached.
