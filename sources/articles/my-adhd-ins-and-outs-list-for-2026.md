#  ADHD Ins and Outs ✅❌

My ADHD Ins and Outs is simply a list of personal habits and focus areas that are linked to adulting with ADHD.

_**‼️Disclaimer: ADHD Ins and Outs are in random order for the thrill of it. I may or may not fulfil them all, but you know what they say, God loves a trier!** _

##  1\. Financial Hygiene💰- IN

Like many people with ADHD, I haven’t had the greatest money habits: impulsive spending, impatience, bad decisions, the list goes on.

I mean, I have an article on **BBC News** on how much money I have lost due to ADHD tax: [ BBC article ADHD Tax ](<https://substack.com/redirect/f7a6c39a-8913-4cd0-b49b-8cd0b73a2beb?j=eyJ1IjoiMzhqd3YifQ.5A9UDKbxtmTMpf48aHOFTZVvvXtwbwJ-khjaPLWh8tA>)

[ ![A screenshot of the BBC article I'm featured in titled 'I lost over £4000 to ADHD Tax'](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!-yM6!,w_354,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F228bef6c-3960-44cb-ae78-5991a5801051_1074x1216.png) ](<https://substack.com/redirect/dc0d94b6-2f89-4574-836c-5bced52e4fdb?j=eyJ1IjoiMzhqd3YifQ.5A9UDKbxtmTMpf48aHOFTZVvvXtwbwJ-khjaPLWh8tA>)

from BBC.com

So I am making this public commitment to over 13,000 of you to improve my financial acumen and habits. **Goodbye ADHD Tax!**

[ ![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vutu!,w_48,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8dcfa1f0-4aeb-476d-858e-4816c1741206_275x198.png) ](<https://substack.com/redirect/2e258199-1b0e-4303-942a-cd75a0fd52fa?j=eyJ1IjoiMzhqd3YifQ.5A9UDKbxtmTMpf48aHOFTZVvvXtwbwJ-khjaPLWh8tA>)

###  _Where to start? 💡_

➡️ I have deleted my saved cards on websites to prevent myself from making impulsive and whimsical spending decisions.

**➡️ To-do list:** Create a monthly budget, monitor outgoings, create a savings system, invest outside of my ISA, and learn more.

🙃 It is quite ironic as I completed at least five financial courses at University and did a short stint working in finance. Ha!

##  2\. Social media, Screen Time, Content Consumption 📲 - OUT

Ok, who am I kidding?

I won’t be abandoning social media; we go together like cheese and crackers 🧀

However, I will make a conscious effort to reduce the amount of content I consume. I think we all should.

⬇️ ⬇️ ⬇️

> _ “We are witnessing people who have never had short attention spans develop shorter attention spans. Unbeknownst to them, aimlessly scrolling, moving from meme to meme, and hyperfixating on reaction videos is causing  **Brain Rot.” -** _ [ Rach Idowu | @AdultingADHD ](<https://open.substack.com/users/6206805-rach-idowu-adultingadhd>)

🎯 I think that consuming Brain Rot content, endlessly scrolling and flipping through social media platforms, can exacerbate ADHD symptoms: **getting easily distracted, lack of attention to detail, impatience, difficulty focusing** , etc.

[ ![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vutu!,w_48,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8dcfa1f0-4aeb-476d-858e-4816c1741206_275x198.png) ](<https://substack.com/redirect/2e258199-1b0e-4303-942a-cd75a0fd52fa?j=eyJ1IjoiMzhqd3YifQ.5A9UDKbxtmTMpf48aHOFTZVvvXtwbwJ-khjaPLWh8tA>)

###  _Where to start? 💡_

➡️ Using the Screen Time feature on my iPhone to set time limits for apps. Yes, it has come to this **🤦🏾‍♀️**

➡️ Doing a social media fast now and again, reading more fiction books, and ignoring rage bait content.

##  3\. Impostor Syndrome 🎭 - OUT

Have you ever:

  * Felt like you didn’t deserve the recognition you were given for your accomplishments?
  * Felt like a fraud even though you have the skills and expertise?
  * Felt like an opportunity is too much for you to handle?



[ ![Orange Vote GIF](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!M9OM!,w_224,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_lossy/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F72a09769-160a-4a8f-8afe-74095522a96f_480x480.gif) ](<https://substack.com/redirect/cf940510-249e-4cf2-9118-5cd8910df21f?j=eyJ1IjoiMzhqd3YifQ.5A9UDKbxtmTMpf48aHOFTZVvvXtwbwJ-khjaPLWh8tA>)

People don’t realise that battling impostor syndrome is rarely a one-time thing.

As we navigate life, new jobs, relationships, and new circumstances, we’ll inevitably experience a level of discomfort or self-doubt.

[ ![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vutu!,w_48,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8dcfa1f0-4aeb-476d-858e-4816c1741206_275x198.png) ](<https://substack.com/redirect/2e258199-1b0e-4303-942a-cd75a0fd52fa?j=eyJ1IjoiMzhqd3YifQ.5A9UDKbxtmTMpf48aHOFTZVvvXtwbwJ-khjaPLWh8tA>)

###  _Where to start? 💡_

**🎯 Perspective** \- Focusing more on what could go right and all of the positives.

**🎯 Acceptance** \- Allowing myself to be nervous but setting a ‘time limit’ to sit with it.

**🎯 Faith** \- Believing that all things will work out for my good - Romans 8:38.

**🎯 Community** \- Opening up to people and asking for words of encouragement.

_“**When it comes to your dreams, you have two choices: pursue them or be haunted by them.”
― Mel Robbins” ** _

##  4\. Adulting With ADHD 🧠 - IN

[ ![Image of a brain emoji playing with a football](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!U9uF!,w_284,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4014cefc-5d7d-4129-84aa-15ffcbfa037f_500x500.png) ](<https://substack.com/redirect/bb07b015-ebbf-44b8-88c9-1742766ffff9?j=eyJ1IjoiMzhqd3YifQ.5A9UDKbxtmTMpf48aHOFTZVvvXtwbwJ-khjaPLWh8tA>)

Graphic from canva

**Is it just me, or has anyone else found themselves overcompensating at times because of their ADHD?**

Even though I’m now able to identify my struggles by way of ADHD traits/symptoms, I find myself overanalysing everything I do and don’t do.

This year, this comes to an end. I’m going to take the pressure off and just be me.

[ ![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vutu!,w_48,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8dcfa1f0-4aeb-476d-858e-4816c1741206_275x198.png) ](<https://substack.com/redirect/2e258199-1b0e-4303-942a-cd75a0fd52fa?j=eyJ1IjoiMzhqd3YifQ.5A9UDKbxtmTMpf48aHOFTZVvvXtwbwJ-khjaPLWh8tA>)

###  | Parting gift 🎁

Look, you’re not a ‘let down’ because you may do things in a way that seems unconventional to the ‘Neurotypical norm’.

A lot of the time, we ADHDers feel like we need to fall in line with the Neurotypical way of doing things, even though our system and method deliver the desired outcome.

**We have no choice but to continue adulting with ADHD, and we can do it.**

**Praying we all have a year filled with happiness, peace and new opportunities!**
