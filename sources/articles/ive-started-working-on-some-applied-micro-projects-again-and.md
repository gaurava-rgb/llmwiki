I’ve started working on some applied micro projects again, and I’ve been making my own set of skills/subagents to work on it

Two ways Compound Engineering has been quite helpful for this

  1. /ce:ideate helps me talk through different aspects of my research process and think about what skills are useful to make, whether potential skill ideas are over engineered, and how they fit into the research flow/with other skills
  2. All the main CE skills are useful for giving CC ideas for how to effectively define subagents and how to delegate to them from skills, and how to optimally design human/AI cooperation (or at least as close to it as I’ve seen in any plugin).



Right now I’m working on doing background reading across decades of legislation/historical documents, creating timelines, etc.

I don’t want to constrain myself to setting my eyes on every word of these docs, so I’d like to have some skill which can decide based on the complexity of a given task whether to do some sort of doc analysis autonomously, or with some Q and A, or recommend I read it.

I’ll update the skills’ flows over time based on what I actually encounter, and prob even have cc update the skill itself by searching through my session history.

Compound Engineering has been moving in a direction of including such “complexity evaluation” in its skills, and this has been very helpful to study (and benefit from) in designing and redesigning my own skills
