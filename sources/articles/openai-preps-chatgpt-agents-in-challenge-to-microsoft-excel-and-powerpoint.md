As [OpenAI](<https://www.theinformation.com/org-charts/openai?rc=c48ukx>) tries to turn ChatGPT into a core application for white-collar work, it’s developing agent features that could make its customers less dependent on productivity apps from [Microsoft](<https://www.theinformation.com/org-charts/microsoft?selected_employee=satya-nadella&rc=c48ukx>).

In the latest example, OpenAI has developed features that let ChatGPT customers quickly create and edit presentations and spreadsheets that are compatible with PowerPoint and Excel directly in the chatbot without using the Microsoft apps themselves, according to a person who has used the new features.

The company has designed buttons below the ChatGPT search bar that direct users to create a spreadsheet or presentation, the person said.

It isn’t clear when OpenAI plans to release the features, but they imply that ChatGPT customers would also be able to download and open these Excel and PowerPoint files using a variety of applications made by companies other than Microsoft.

Anyone can create files that are compatible with Excel and PowerPoint because Microsoft has made the formats for those files open source, so OpenAI doesn’t need Microsoft’s permission to do so.

The new ChatGPT agents also will also help customers generate reports based on corporate or public data, or handle repetitive tasks involving websites, such as scheduling and booking appointments.

Taken together, these productivity features could make ChatGPT even more attractive as a business tool, posing a threat to productivity suites sold by the likes of Microsoft and Google. (Ironically, ChatGPT runs almost entirely on Microsoft servers due to the companies’ financial arrangement.)

Hundreds of millions of people use ChatGPT, including tens of millions of paying subscribers, and OpenAI wants to make the app a gateway through which consumers and enterprises use online services or get work done.

An AI agent is technology that goes beyond what the basic version of ChatGPT can do, handling tasks that take multiple steps, without requiring a human to steer the whole process. (There are roughly [seven types of AI agents](<https://www.theinformation.com/articles/seven-kinds-ai-agents>).)

A core feature of traditional productivity apps is the ability for multiple customers to work together on documents or other files. OpenAI has developed but hasn’t released similar collaboration tools allowing multiple ChatGPT customers to chat about or work together on documents in the app, [The Information has reported](<https://www.theinformation.com/articles/openai-quietly-designed-rival-google-workspace-microsoft-office>). It isn’t clear when the company plans to release them.

[](<https://www.theinformation.com/deep-research>)

[Millions of corporate employees](<https://www.theinformation.com/articles/openai-starts-selling-chatgpt-discount-hurting-microsoft>) already use ChatGPT individually or as part of enterprise subscription plans to create reports, generate or edit text for marketing copy, analyze corporate data and produce computer code to develop applications.

But most businesses prefer to buy suites of apps that can handle work for many types of employees. Microsoft and Google have released artificial intelligence features for their productivity apps, mimicking the kinds of text or image generation people do with ChatGPT. That is [leading customers to spend slightly more money on the software](<https://www.theinformation.com/articles/microsofts-move-make-people-pay-ai-starts-pay>) even as some businesses [have complained the features don’t always work perfectly](<https://www.theinformation.com/articles/microsoft-customers-pause-on-office-ai-assistant-due-to-budgets-bugs?rc=geicgp>).

ChatGPT’s offering may not suit many people. For instance, it lacks a file storage system like those found in Google Workspace or Microsoft Office for organizing files in folders.

The new ChatGPT agents can also be slow or buggy, said the person who has used them. It can often take an agent a half hour or more to complete tasks a human could do in 10 to 15 minutes, they said.

Still, the agent features, which are analogous to asking an intern to handle menial tasks, can save customers time, this person said.

Spokespeople for OpenAI and Microsoft didn’t have a comment.

**Cash Flow Analysis**

The new ChatGPT capabilities for generating reports or traversing websites are similar to existing ChatGPT features: Deep Research and Operator.

Deep Research essentially works by reading and summarizing webpages. The upcoming Reports agent takes this a step further by interacting with those webpages. For instance, it can click on links in articles to read other related stories, and it can conduct searches in online databases, said the person who has used the product.

Compared to Operator, the new Actions agent is less likely to get stuck and is better at trying different approaches when it runs into issues like a blocked website, the person who has used the product said.

The upcoming Spreadsheets and Presentations agents represent a brand-new capability for ChatGPT. Customers can generate and edit Excel spreadsheets and PowerPoint presentations using natural language prompts such as “Build me a discounted cash flow analysis for Apple using publicly available financial documents,” or “Make a presentation showing how TSMC’s capital expenditure costs have changed over time.”

These agents work by writing code that generates a file compatible with PowerPoint and Excel, said the person who has used them.
