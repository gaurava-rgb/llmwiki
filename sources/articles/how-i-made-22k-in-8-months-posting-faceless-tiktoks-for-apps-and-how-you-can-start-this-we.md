## Some context first

I started this as a side hustle while working full time. I was scrolling tiktok one night and kept seeing the same type of content over and over - faceless videos promoting apps with voiceovers like "i found this app that..." or looksmaxing videos having an end CTA and the app's rating mid video.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HBIeEB8a4AEzTfl.jpg)

I commented on one asking how they made it and they told me about content rewards, this platform where brands post campaigns and clippers get paid per view. that's where this whole thing started.

Clipping = brands/agencies post campaigns on content rewards with specific angles and formats, you create the content following their guidelines, post it from your own accounts, submit your view counts through the platform, get paid per view.

Most campaigns pay around $1 per 1,000 views (called RPM or rate per mille). some pay more, some pay less depending on the niche and how competitive it is.

The model is simple: views = direct money. if your video gets 50k views you make $50. if it gets 200 views you make $0.20. all the risk is on you to make content that performs.

## **What clipping actually is**

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HBI710OaEAAEjus.jpg)

_here's how it works from start to finish:_

You join content rewards and browse active campaigns. brands post what they need: the app/product you're promoting, the content angle they want, the format (slideshow, voiceover, screen recording, etc), and what they're paying per view.

You apply to campaigns that match your accounts. some campaigns are open to everyone, some require you to submit examples first. Content rewards handles all the approvals so you know exactly which campaigns you're cleared for.

You make the content following their angle. most campaigns give you exact scripts or talking points. like "show yourself struggling with [problem] then reveal [app] as the solution" or "do a before/after comparison using [feature]."

You post from your own TikTok/Instagram accounts. not the brand's account - YOUR account. that's the whole point. it looks like organic content from a real person, not an ad.

You track your views and submit them through content rewards. The platform has built-in tracking so you just connect your accounts and it pulls the data automatically. Way easier than manually screenshotting everything.

You get paid through content rewards' payment system. they handle payouts via paypal, stripe, or crypto depending on your preference. payment terms vary by campaign but most pay within 7-14 days after views are verified.

The content is "faceless" meaning you don't show your face on camera. it's screen recordings, slideshow videos with text overlays, stock footage with voiceovers, product demos, comparison videos - anything that doesn't require you to be on camera.

This makes it way easier to scale because you're not tied to your personal brand. you can run multiple accounts, try different niches, and create content faster since you're not filming yourself.

**3\. CapCut** \- free video editing app. this is where i make probably 70% of my content. The free version is not fine anymore since it has a watermark so I use the pro version.

**4\. Canva** \- for creating slideshow images and thumbnails. I have the pro version ($13/month) but you can start with free. lets me access their stock photos and templates which speeds everything up.

**5\. Elevenlabs** \- ai voiceover tool. $5/month for the starter plan. i use this for any content that needs a voiceover instead of just text on screen. sounds way more natural than TikTok's text-to-speech.

**6\. Proxy** \- basically a better version of VPN, it's static and won't change the location, meaning I don't get shadowbans and I always get 90% USA Audience

That's it. total cost if you're starting from scratch: maybe $20/month for the essentials Capcut Pro and Proxy. everything else is free or takes a small cut from earnings you're already making.

## Content formats that actually work

Slideshows (2-6 images with text): easiest to make, consistently get 5k-30k views if the hook is good. takes 20-30 minutes.

Brainrot quiz videos: gets alot of engagement because there is always CTA's mid video, takes 1h to make and averages 5k-2m if the video is controversial and good. Usually you don't see brands promoting their app or brand with this format but there are couple brands that have integrated their app with this exact niche;

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HBIjl3-bwAAf-MT.png)

Before/After comparisons: works for apps with visual results. consistently get 10k-40k views. takes 25 minutes.

Pov/Storytime style: "pov you need to [solve problem]" then show the app. hit or miss but when they hit they really hit. takes 20 minutes.

**what doesn't work:**

  * anything that looks like an ad
  * low effort reposts
  * content over 1 min

## How to start

join @contentrewards and create an account. browse the campaign marketplace.

set up tiktok and instagram accounts and warm the accounts up.

pick your first campaign:

  * pays at least $0.5 per 1k views
  * clear content angles
  * niche you understand
  * beginner-friendly



make your first video using the formula i shared. don't overthink it. your first video will probably be mid. post it anyway.

post 7x per week minimum. Content Rewards automatically tracks your views. you just tag which campaign each video is for.

get paid once views are verified (usually 7 days). cash out when you hit minimum.

scale by adding more accounts, more campaigns, or better tools and editors.
