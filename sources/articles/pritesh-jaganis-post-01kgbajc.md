A job seeker in my community wasted 6 weeks applying to the same jobs as everyone else. He spammed easy apply, and every job he would see on LinkedIn looked like a ghost job been there for ages.

The first time I got to know about him, I gave him a way to find 20+ job listings daily instead of the daily ghost jobs that 1000s of candidates see.

Btw, I run a community of 5100+ jobseekers, my Job Hunting Accelerator, where I've regularly given this advice to so many people.

We've got modules for networking, job hunting strategy, and have tons of guest sessions as well. Check it out here: [https://lnkd.in/gt5YBAbr](<https://www.linkedin.com/redir/redirect?url=https%3A%2F%2Flnkd.in%2Fgt5YBAbr&urlhash=QzFD&trk=public_post-text>)

You see, companies post jobs on their career pages before LinkedIn.
Sometimes hours before. Sometimes days.

And almost nobody is looking there.

Last Tuesday at 9 AM, I found a Product Manager role at a Series B startup. Posted 3 hours earlier. 2 applicants when I saw it.

That same role hit LinkedIn Thursday afternoon.
847 applicants by Friday.

I could’ve landed the interview, and those 845 other people would still be waiting.

Here's what I figured out:

Most companies use the same handful of job platforms.
Workday, Greenhouse, Lever, iCIMS, SmartRecruiters.

Google knows exactly where they are. Here’s a search that finds every new posting across these platforms in seconds.

The base search:

("your job title") (site:[myworkdayjobs.com](<https://www.linkedin.com/redir/redirect?url=http%3A%2F%2Fmyworkdayjobs.com&urlhash=d1gA&trk=public_post-text>) OR site:[greenhouse.io](<https://www.linkedin.com/redir/redirect?url=http%3A%2F%2Fgreenhouse.io&urlhash=lbr4&trk=public_post-text>) OR site:[lever.co](<https://www.linkedin.com/redir/redirect?url=http%3A%2F%2Flever.co&urlhash=YUnl&trk=public_post-text>) OR site:[smartrecruiters.com](<https://www.linkedin.com/redir/redirect?url=http%3A%2F%2Fsmartrecruiters.com&urlhash=M95O&trk=public_post-text>) OR site:[icims.com](<https://www.linkedin.com/redir/redirect?url=http%3A%2F%2Ficims.com&urlhash=F1ik&trk=public_post-text>))

That's it. Replace "your job title" with whatever you're looking for.

The game changer is sorting by recency. After you run the search, look at the URL in your browser. Add this to the very end:

&tbs=qdr:d

Now you only see jobs posted in the last day.

Do this every morning. Takes 20 minutes.

Find 10-15 fresh postings.
Scan them fast.

Apply to the best 3-4 before anyone else even knows they exist.

His results after switching:

Week 1: 12 applications, 3 responses
Week 2: 15 applications, 5 responses
Week 3: 18 applications, 6 responses
Week 4: 2 offers

LinkedIn posts take time to populate. By the time you see them, hundreds of people already applied. Hiring managers are buried. Your resume goes to the bottom of a pile that never gets fully reviewed.

Direct ATS applications land when the inbox is empty.
You're applicant number 4, not applicant 432.
