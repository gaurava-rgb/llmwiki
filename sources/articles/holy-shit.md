Holy shit... Google just killed the "I don't have a GPU" excuse forever.

Google Colab now runs directly inside VS Code.

Free T4 GPU. Your local files. Google's servers.

Here's everything you need to know: 👇

Your browser does not support the video tag.

* * *

For years, devs had to choose:

→ VS Code (your setup, your extensions, your workflow)

→ Colab (free GPU, cloud compute, model training)

You couldn't have both.

Google just fixed that.

* * *

Free tier includes:

\- NVIDIA T4 GPU (16GB VRAM)

\- TPU v5e access

\- Your IntelliSense, Git, debugger -- all still working

\- Zero CUDA installs. Zero driver conflicts.

Pro tier unlocks A100s.

* * *

Real benchmark from LoRA fine-tuning on 1,000 samples:

CPU: 50 min/epoch

T4 GPU: 3 min/epoch

That's a 16x speedup.

For free.

* * *

Setup takes 2 minutes:

  1. Install "Google Colab" extension in VS Code
  2. Click "Select Kernel" → "Colab"
  3. Sign in with Google
  4. Pick your GPU/TPU
  5. Done -- you're running on cloud hardware



No browser tab. No random disconnects.

Every dev now has access to the same compute as researchers at top labs.
