I spoke to a PM at Coinbase last week.
Working 14-hour days.
Possibly getting put on a PIP. Burned out.

Her question: "Can I still do your program on weekends only?"

I told her the truth.

The company is already deciding your future without you. The question is whether you're doing the same.

Here's what I've learned after coaching 500+ PMs through this exact moment:

The uncertainty you're feeling right now? It's actually data.

You already have access to more information than most. You see the roadmap. You know the quarterly numbers. You know which bets the company is making and which are quietly dying.

So stop asking "is the market okay?" and start asking "is my company okay?"

Pull the numbers. Look at revenue direction, customer growth, headcount trends. Talk to senior PMs about where the priorities are shifting. Build a simple model.

If the trajectory is clear, you have your answer. And if it's not clear, that's also your answer.

Then make a decision.

Not a panicked one. A strategic one.

Because here's the part most PMs miss: not looking doesn't mean you're safe. It just means you're reacting later, with less time and fewer options.

The PMs I see land $300K-$500K+ roles at Google, Meta, and Amazon? They didn't wait for the layoff email.

They treated their career like a product. With a roadmap, milestones, and defended calendar time to actually work on it.

One hour a day. That's it. Block it on your calendar as "Project X" starting next week.

Your resume, your LinkedIn, your story bank. It doesn't have to be perfect. It just has to start.

Are you proactively managing your career right now, or waiting to see how things shake out?

Curious where most people are.

Most PMs wait for the PIP or layoff email to start looking. But if you're not treating your career like a product with a roadmap, you're already behind.
