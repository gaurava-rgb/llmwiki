Most people using AI are working harder than they need to.

Not because they're lazy. Not because they picked the wrong tool. But because nobody told them there are two completely different ways to use AI, and only one of them actually changes your life.

# The way most people use AI

You have a task. You open ChatGPT, Claude, or whatever you use. You type out what you need. It gives you something. You maybe edit it. You close the tab.

Tomorrow, you do it again. Same process. New prompt.

That's prompting. And it's fine. It's genuinely useful. But it's not the thing people are talking about when they say AI changed their life. Prompting saves you minutes. What we're talking about saves you days.

# The shift: from prompting to delegating

**Prompting** is using AI like a faster version of Google. You ask, it answers, you move on.

**Delegating** is different. You hand over an entire responsibility. Not a task, a system. The AI figures out the steps, runs the process, and delivers the output. Repeatedly. Without you having to think about it again.

One is a tool you pick up.

The other is a team member you brief once.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HCuNeZMXgAANNk7.jpg)

# Step 1: Audit your week

Before you can delegate, you need to know what's worth delegating.

Open your AI and send it this:


    Here's how I typically spend my week: [describe it honestly].

    I want you to identify the 5 tasks that are the most repetitive, follow a predictable pattern, or require research and synthesis.

    Rank them by time wasted per week.


Most people who do this are genuinely surprised. Tasks they thought required their full attention turn out to be completely automatable. Your AI will surface them fast.

# Step 2: Turn tasks into standing orders

This is the actual shift.

Instead of prompting your AI every time you need something, you build it a standing order. A recurring instruction it can follow without you.

Here's the difference in practice:

Prompting version: "Hey, what's trending in crypto marketing this week?"

Delegating version:


    Every Monday morning, scan the top 10 accounts in crypto marketing, the last 7 days of posts from [list], and the latest from [newsletters].

    Pull the 3 most relevant trends for someone running a marketing agency.

    Format it as a briefing I can read in under 3 minutes.

    Flag anything I should act on.


You write that once. It runs forever.

# Step 3: Give your AI context about your life

Your AI is only as powerful as what it knows about you. A new hire with zero context is useless. A teammate who knows your goals, your style, your schedule, and your standards is invaluable.

Take 20 minutes and paste in:

  * What you're trying to achieve this month
  * Your recurring tasks and how you currently handle them
  * What good output looks like for you
  * The decisions you want it to make vs the ones it should flag for you



This is the difference between AI that feels generic and AI that feels like it actually knows your business.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HCuNjoKXsAARdDM.jpg)

# What this looks like when it's working

**Content creation:** Your AI monitors trends daily, surfaces what's worth writing about based on your niche and audience, and drafts a first version you can edit in 10 minutes instead of starting from scratch.

**YouTube:** It analyzes what's performing in your space, identifies outlier videos and why they worked, generates ideas tailored to your channel, and gives you a script structure to record from.

**Sales and outreach:** It scans for new leads that match your ideal client profile, ranks them by fit, writes personalized first messages based on their recent activity, and keeps a running log of where every conversation stands.

These aren't hypotheticals. This is what's running in the background for people who've made the shift.

# The prompt that starts everything

If you take nothing else from this, take this:


    I want to completely delegate [task] to you.

    Ask me everything you need to know to run this without me having to prompt you each time.

    Then build me a repeatable system I can hand back to you every time this comes up.


Send this for one task this week. See what it builds. Then do it again next week for another one.

That's the whole playbook.

# The math is simple

Most people will use AI thousands of times this year. Every single interaction a one-off prompt. Same effort, slightly better output.

The ones who figure out delegation will have built systems that run their research, their content, their outreach, and their admin while they focus on the work only they can do.

40 hours of output. 10 hours of actual work.
