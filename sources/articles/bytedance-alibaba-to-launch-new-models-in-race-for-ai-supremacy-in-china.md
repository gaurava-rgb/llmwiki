The battle for AI dominance in China is reaching new heights. Tech giants ByteDance and Alibaba Group are both poised to release their next flagship AI models around the Lunar New Year holiday in mid-February, in what could be the most consequential battle in the country’s tech sector.

[ByteDance](<https://www.theinformation.com/org-charts/bytedance>) is preparing to release three new AI models—a large language model, an image generation model and a video model—next month, according to two people with direct knowledge of the matter. The planned launch comes as the TikTok owner’s fast-growing cloud computing business has used its AI services to [challenge Alibaba,](<https://www.theinformation.com/articles/bytedance-challenges-alibabas-reign-chinas-ai-cloud-market?rc=9byxri>) whose cloud arm is the biggest in China. But Alibaba isn’t sitting still.

### The Takeaway

  *   *   *


Alibaba aims to launch the new generation of its flagship AI models during the same holiday period, according to two other people with direct knowledge of its plan. The models offer strong math and coding capabilities, the people said. The company also plans to make a big holiday marketing push for its Qwen AI app for consumers, in a challenge to ByteDance’s popular Doubao chatbot, China’s largest AI app by users, according to the people.

The competition could be even fiercer given that DeepSeek is also planning to release its next big model around the same time, after a year without doing so, [The Information reported](<https://www.theinformation.com/articles/deepseek-release-next-flagship-ai-model-strong-coding-ability?rc=jnr9wn>) previously.

DeepSeek became a global sensation during the Lunar New Year holidays a year ago, when the vast majority of people in the country took off for a week and with ample screen time to consume news and entertainment content. This year, both Alibaba and ByteDance are planning to take advantage of the same window as they battle to make their AI apps the most popular among consumers in China.

ByteDance is preparing to launch Doubao 2.0, its new flagship large language model, Seedream 5.0, an image generation model, and SeedDance 2.0, a video generation model. Alibaba, meanwhile, plans to roll out its new flagship model, Qwen 3.5, which is specifically optimized for complex reasoning tasks.

ByteDance has a slight edge in chatbot apps thanks to the popularity of Doubao, which had 163 million monthly active users as of last month, according to [Aicpb.com](<http://aicpb.com/>), a Chinese website that tracks AI products. ByteDance has integrated the Doubao chatbot into its Douyin video app, the Chinese sister of TikTok and the country’s biggest short-video app that has also evolved into a major e-commerce platform.

[](<https://www.theinformation.com/deep-research>)

But Alibaba has fought back. In November, the company launched the Qwen app, a makeover of its previous lackluster Tongyi chatbot app and powered by its latest [Qwen 3.0 model](<https://www.theinformation.com/articles/alibaba-helped-china-take-lead-u-s-open-source-ai?rc=jnr9wn>). Earlier this month, Alibaba said it would connect Qwen with the company’s e-commerce, online travel and affiliate Ant Group’s payment platforms, in a major upgrade that enables the chatbot to serve as an all-encompassing agent that helps users with real-life tasks like ordering food online and booking trips.

The Qwen app is the centerpiece of Alibaba’s AI strategy and the company’s internal target is to complete the integration of all Alibaba ecosystem services into the app during the first half of 2026, according to Alibaba employees with knowledge of the plan. The Qwen app now has more than 100 million monthly active users.

ByteDance and Alibaba didn’t comment.

**Battle for 1.4 Billion Users**

The high-stakes rivalry between ByteDance and Alibaba could shape how China’s 1.4 billion people will use AI in all aspects of their everyday activities in the coming years, and the winner could also control China’s AI cloud market that JPMorgan projects will reach nearly $90 billion by 2030.

Apart from their upcoming models, both ByteDance and Alibaba are also working on ambitious long-term projects to develop an all-in-one AI model that not only processes but also generates text, images, audio, video, and code seamlessly, according to three people with direct knowledge of the projects.

Other smaller startups in China are also developing competitive AI models. In addition to DeepSeek, Moonshot AI, another LLM startup, earlier this month [released a new model Kimi K2.5](<https://www.theinformation.com/briefings/chinas-moonshot-ai-releases-new-open-source-model-kimi-k2-5?rc=5pjqe5>), which boasts capabilities in coding as well as its understanding of visual data. But unlike ByteDance and Alibaba, those upstart competitors don’t have their own cloud platforms or huge user base that could fund the model development.

In China, the only other giant that has the resources to take on both ByteDance and Alibaba in the AI race is Tencent Holdings, whose WeChat messaging app has more than 1.4 billion monthly active users. But Tencent has so far lagged in AI model development compared to the two rivals.

Over the past several months, Tencent has been stepping up its effort to catch up, by [rebuilding its AI team](<https://www.theinformation.com/articles/tencent-poaches-bytedance-researchers-china-ai-race-heats?preview=true&rc=jnr9wn>) under its new chief AI scientist Yao Shunyu, who joined from [OpenAI](<https://www.theinformation.com/org-charts/openai>) in September. At Tencent’s annual staff meeting earlier this week, CEO Pony Ma acknowledged that the firm had moved too slowly, but he also emphasized that it is building a stronger AI team, according to two Tencent employees who attended the meeting.
