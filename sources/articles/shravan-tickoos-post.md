So I just wrote an 800-page book for every non-technical person who is afraid of AI and wishes they could learn it ❤️

It is free. Here is why.

For years, as a product manager, I sat in rooms with engineers and pretended I understood.

I nodded at "microservices." Smiled at "CI/CD." Faked confidence about "deploying to production."

For ten years, that worked. Until it didn't.

Last year, I opened a terminal for the first time. Black screen. Blinking cursor. The thing I had been scared of my entire career.

I typed one command. Asked an AI to help me build something.
24 hours later — a full-stack app. Auth. Database. 26 pages. Deployed. Live.

The same me. The guy who could not write a for-loop.

I sat on my bed shaking. Not because it felt unreal — but because it was possible. And I had spent ten years believing it wasn't.

After that, I couldn't stop. Multi-agent AI pipelines. A RAG system over Vedic texts with 161,000 vectors and a cloned voice. An automated
video pipeline. An open-source Knowledge OS. An LMS serving hundreds of students.

All by the "non-technical" person afraid to open a terminal twelve months ago.

Then at 2 AM it hit me: there are millions like me.

PMs understanding 40% of stand-ups. Founders waiting months for a
developer. Designers praying implementations match mockups. Anyone who
wants to learn AI but feels intimidated.

Brilliant minds locked out of the biggest shift in history, not because they cannot learn, but because nobody explained it clearly.

So I wrote The Builder's Bible, with Claude as my co-author.

34 chapters. 4 hands-on projects. 198,000 words. Insights inspired by Karpathy, Chip Huyen, Eugene Yan, Jay Alammar, Martin Kleppmann.

With How the internet works.
How databases store memory.
How LLMs reason.
How RAG, agents, and MCP function.
How to build chatbots, dashboards, multi-agent systems.
System design. Security. The 2026 frontier.

Every concept follows ARIA — my teaching framework:
Analogy → Real-life example → Intuition → Actual explanation in plain language.

No jargon without context. No "obviously." No "simply."

I brought years of product experience across Flipkart and BlackBuck, and lessons from mentoring 10,000+ PMs. Claude brought synthesis from 25+ sources into structured clarity.

Releasing it completely free. No paywall. No email gate.

Because the 2015 IIT Roorkee graduate terrified of code deserved this book. Nobody gave it to him.
