[ Jim Cook ](<https://www.linkedin.com/in/cookflix?trk=public_post_feed-actor-name>)

BenchBoard Coaching & Exec. Advisory "Cook’s PlayBooks"; Alliance CEO’s Director; Operators Guild; Mozilla (Firefox; 2005); Netflix Founding Team (1997-1999); early Intuit Team and IPO (1991-1996)

A few weeks ago I attended the a16z Finance Demo Day hosted by [Ivan Makarov](<https://www.linkedin.com/in/ivansmakarov?trk=public_post-text>) and [Seema Amble](<https://www.linkedin.com/in/seema-amble-b404126?trk=public_post-text>) where the audience did some deep dives on a handful of the next generation of the new finance stack being built.

[Runway](<https://www.linkedin.com/company/runwayml?trk=public_post-text>), [Equals](<https://www.linkedin.com/company/equalshq?trk=public_post-text>), [Sequence](<https://uk.linkedin.com/company/sequence-hq?trk=public_post-text>), [Concourse](<https://www.linkedin.com/company/concourseai?trk=public_post-text>), and [Metronome](<https://www.linkedin.com/company/getmetronome?trk=public_post-text>), each product showed what’s possible when real-time data and clean UX collide with legacy finance workflows.

But it was a post-event conversation with [Scott Woody](<https://www.linkedin.com/in/scott-woody-21677121?trk=public_post-text>), CEO of [Metronome](<https://www.linkedin.com/company/getmetronome?trk=public_post-text>), that inspired this post.

We weren’t just talking about shaving days off the accounting close.

We were talking about re-architecting the close entirely.

So I asked Scott and the group standing nearby a first-principles question:

👉 What’s the actual purpose of an accounting close anyway?

Scott replied - “Yes, we are the verge of a continuous close”. Nobody in that room gave the old school answer of “We’ve always done it that way.” Or “It’s GAAP.”

For visionary finance leaders like [Scott Woody](<https://www.linkedin.com/in/scott-woody-21677121?trk=public_post-text>), [Siqi Chen](<https://www.linkedin.com/in/siqic?trk=public_post-text>), [Matthieu Hafemeister](<https://www.linkedin.com/in/matthafemeister?trk=public_post-text>) and the others, those are terrible answers.

The real goal is consistent, accurate, and faster numbers to make faster decisions? Hard stop. The real question then is “How consistent and how accurate do we really need to be to run the business?”

What if the new Finance AI stack isn’t just about automation but about rethinking the entire people-process-system tech from scratch?

What if:
→ We could deliver daily, decision-ready numbers
→ We kept the “C” for Compliance behind the closed doors of finance, and opened the finance doors for Continuous, Contextual, and Confident set of numbers?

The bottom line:
Let’s open the books and re-imagine the close.
