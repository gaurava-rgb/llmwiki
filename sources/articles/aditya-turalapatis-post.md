If you can’t Build with AI, you won’t get hired is the new AI PM reality

A year or two ago, product interviews were predictable.
\- Product design.
\- Estimation.
\- Execution trade offs.
\- Metrics & RCA
\- Behavioral

You were tested on structured thinking and clarity.
You did not have to build.
You just had to reason well.

Now, AI PM interviews feel very different and are additionally testing on these execution and handson skills

[#VibeCoding](<https://www.linkedin.com/signup/cold-join?session_redirect=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.linkedin.com%2Ffeed%2Fhashtag%2Fvibecoding&trk=public_post-text>) Round
You build live using tools like GitHub Copilot, OpenAI, Gemini, Anthropic.
They watch:
* How you iterate when outputs are bad
* How you refine prompts
* How you reason about hallucinations
* Trade offs across UX, latency, cost, safety
* Whether you are comfortable shipping imperfect systems
Talking is not enough. You have to ship.

[#Evals](<https://www.linkedin.com/signup/cold-join?session_redirect=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.linkedin.com%2Ffeed%2Fhashtag%2Fevals&trk=public_post-text>) and Quality Round
Now they go deeper:
* How you design offline eval datasets
* What online metrics you track
* How you detect regressions
* What failed in production
* How you improved quality over time
AI PMs are expected to think in probabilities, not binaries.

[#Scale](<https://www.linkedin.com/signup/cold-join?session_redirect=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.linkedin.com%2Ffeed%2Fhashtag%2Fscale&trk=public_post-text>) and Strategy Round
This is judgment.
* Should this even use AI
* How do you build enterprise trust
* How does cost behave at scale
* How does it fit into a broader platform
* How do you drive durable adoption

The shift is clear
Then: Structured thinker
Now: Product builder + evaluator + systems thinker
The bar is higher
But so is the impact
