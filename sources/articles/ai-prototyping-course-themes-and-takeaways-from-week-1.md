Kicked off Week 1 of my Maven course on AI prototyping, and the chat/Q&A were gold.

Three big themes:
1️⃣ AI is now a normal part of the product toolkit
People shared using AI for ideation, research synthesis, and quick mocks. Now it’s just normal that you’ve used AI somewhere in your work, and lines are blurring between roles, especially for AI features.

2️⃣ Process questions > tool questions
The most useful Q&A wasn’t “which tool?” but “how should I use it?”. We discussed when to use the discuss and plan modes and how to get started without a design system.

3️⃣ Multiple variants beat one precious mock
We did an interactive exercise to brainstorm different AI ideas for the same business problem, and then prototype these as variants on a landing page. Students were excited about how to use this approach to accelerate finding the best solution, not just shipping it fast.

This week’s homework: ship one landing page + multiple variants for a real problem.

If you’re curious how we’re structuring AI prototyping workflows (discuss/plan mode → pick variants → landing page), comment and let me know. I might do a breakdown post and share the custom GPT I made for this.
