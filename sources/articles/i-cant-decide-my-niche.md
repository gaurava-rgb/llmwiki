'I can't decide my niche'

Bro, just read this

![Turn Any Niche Into Your Own](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HB2jliXaEAAjSgO.jpg)

most people make the mistake of trying to "find" a niche as if it's a lottery ticket. scroll youtube long enough and the right one will appear. it doesn't work that way.

the most successful channels don't choose niches, they carve out their own. if you're struggling to stand out, it's not because your niche is too small. it's because your angle isn't unique enough.

## stop thinking niche. start thinking angle.

niches are crowded. angles are unlimited. the difference matters more than most people realize.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HB2i8HGboAA4nI2.png)

_your angle is what makes you unique. it's how you stand out without competing with everyone else. niches are a commodity. angles are a moat._

## "saturated" niches are secret goldmines

most people see a saturated niche and think "it's too crowded. i can't compete." wrong. crowded niches prove demand exists. the key is positioning yourself differently inside them.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HB2jDJfa0AAG3yK.png)

stop running from competition. find a way to position yourself differently within a crowded space. competition is proof the audience exists.

## the 3-step formula for making any niche yours

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HB2jPyTaYAAQVhB.png)

## find your one-sentence niche

if you can't explain your channel in one sentence, it's too broad. this formula forces clarity.

## if you discover a new audience, study them deeply

when you find an outlier niche or unexpected audience, don't just copy the format. go deeper.

study the comment section to find what people love about it. study the audience pain points. find the specific emotion they're feeling when they click. what frustration brought them there? what hope are they looking for?

with this you can not only copy the niche or the angle — you can create a completely new sub-niche, because you know the audience is hungry for more and you understand exactly what they want from it.

## what youtube insiders know that most don't

→

**Niching down doesn't shrink your audience**

it makes your content more binge-worthy. a hyper-specific channel pulls in people who feel like it was made exactly for them. that's retention. that's subscribers.

→

**YouTube doesn't reward "good content"**

it rewards retention and repeat viewers. if your angle doesn't bring people back for the next video, you lose. being good isn't enough.

→

**Most people waste time "finding their niche"**

the real winners create demand by framing a niche their own way. you're not discovering a niche. you're building one.

→

**Clickbait isn't the problem. boring content is.**

you can get clicks once. but if your angle doesn't make people return, the channel dies. the hook gets them in. the angle keeps them coming back.

→

**The best channels don't just educate or entertain**

they create a specific emotional experience that keeps people addicted. what emotion does your channel produce? if you can't name it, you don't have an angle yet.

your niche isn't something you find. it's something you create. most people are waiting to be discovered. the ones who win are the ones who build their own lane.
